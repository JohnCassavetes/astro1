## Key Findings

### 1. Pilot Sample Enrichment
The pilot batch (596 galaxies) had a **1.2% detection rate** while the expanded sample had **0%**. Possible explanations:
- Pilot sample was randomly selected and happened to be rich in peculiar galaxies
- Expanded sample galaxies were fainter/homogeneous in morphology
- Detection threshold was too conservative for expanded sample characteristics

### 2. Novelty Filtering Effectiveness
- 239 anomalies flagged by Isolation Forest
- Top 100 examined via novelty filtering
- **7 passed all filters** (SIMBAD, NED, literature)
- **86% of anomalies were previously discussed** in literature
- **12% were known objects** (recovered)
- **2% were artifacts**

### 3. Spectroscopic Status
SDSS SkyServer queries confirm **none of the top 3 candidates have existing spectroscopic observations**.
